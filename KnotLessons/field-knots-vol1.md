# Field Knots — Vol. I: A Working Repertoire

Ten knots sorted by job: stoppers, loops, hitches, bends, bindings. A knot is chosen by its job, not its name. Written steps consolidate a knot rather than teach it cold — watch each once ([Animated Knots by Grog](https://www.animatedknots.com/)), then drill from this page.

## Rope words

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Working end | The end you're actively tying with |
| Standing part | The rest of the rope — under load, left alone |
| Bight | A U-shaped bend that doesn't cross itself |
| Loop | A circle where the rope does cross itself |
| Turn / round turn | Once around an object; a round turn wraps it twice, fully |

## Stoppers — stop a rope pulling through

### Overhand knot *(foundation)*
The atom of knotting. Loop near the end → working end through the loop → tighten.
**Mind:** jams hard under load; hence the figure-eight replaces it as the working stopper.

### Figure-eight *(core)*
Bulkier stopper, unties easily after load.
1. Loop near the end. 2. Working end goes **behind** the standing part (the extra half-twist is the whole difference from an overhand). 3. Forward and down through the loop. 4. Dress to a clean "8".
**Check:** if it looks like an 8 it's right.

## Loops — a fixed eye that won't slip

### [Bowline](https://www.animatedknots.com/?s=bowline) *(core)*
Fixed loop at the end of a line: strong, non-slipping, unties easily.
1. Small loop in the standing part (the "hole"); standing part runs away as the "tree". 2. Rabbit up out of the hole, 3. round the back of the tree, 4. back down the hole. 5. Set against the standing part.
**Mnemonic:** rabbit out of the hole, round the tree, back down the hole.
**Mind:** can shake loose unloaded — leave a long tail; back up the tail for anything safety-critical.

### Figure-eight on a bight *(core)*
Fixed loop mid-rope or at the end; easy to inspect at a glance.
1. Fold a long bight. 2. Tie a figure-eight in the doubled rope as if one strand. 3. Dress to clean parallel strands.
**Mind:** can weld shut after shock load — its one weakness against the bowline.

## Hitches — fix a rope to a post, ring or rail

### [Clove hitch](https://www.animatedknots.com/?s=clove+hitch) *(core)*
Fast, adjustable attachment; the start of most lashings.
1. Over and around the post. 2. Cross the first wrap, around again slightly higher. 3. Tuck the working end under the second wrap, alongside the first — the crossings form an X. 4. Pull both ends.
**Check:** an X on the post means a clove.
**Mind:** slips under steady one-sided pull; a positioning knot, not a mooring.

### Round turn and two half hitches *(core)*
The reliable workhorse: tie a loaded line off and trust it overnight.
1. Twice fully around the post — the round turn carries the load while you tie. 2. Working end over the standing part and through: one half hitch. 3. Repeat **the same direction**: second half hitch. 4. Snug down.
**Mind:** both hitches same way round, or it degrades.

### Taut-line hitch *(core)*
Adjustable hitch that grips under load: guy-lines, ridgelines.
1. Around the anchor and back. 2. Two wraps around the standing part **inside** the loop, working toward the anchor. 3. One more wrap **outside** the loop; tuck under itself. 4. Slide to tension; it grips when loaded.
**Mind:** two inside, one outside. Slick cord may need a third inside wrap.

## Bends — join two ropes

### Sheet bend *(core)*
Joins two ropes, including unequal thicknesses — which almost nothing else does well.
1. Bight in the **thicker** rope. 2. Thinner rope up through the bight, 3. around behind both legs, 4. tucked back under its own standing part — not through the bight again. 5. Both short ends finish on the **same side**.
**Mind:** ends on opposite sides = left-handed sheet bend, markedly weaker. Slippery or very unequal ropes: go round twice (double sheet bend).
**Kinship:** structurally a bowline tied between two ropes.

## Bindings — tie a bundle shut

### Reef knot *(caution)*
Binds a parcel or bandage against itself. Left over right and tuck; right over left and tuck; should lie flat and symmetrical.
**Mnemonic:** left over right, then right over left (same hand twice = granny knot).
**Mind:** never use to join two ropes under load — it capsizes and spills. It binds; it does not bend.

## Systems — knots in combination

### Trucker's hitch *(compound)*
Rope-only pulley, roughly 3:1 — lashing loads, drum-tight ridgelines.
1. Fix the far end (round turn and two half hitches). 2. Mid-line, tie a small fixed loop (figure-eight on a bight, or slipped loop for quick release). 3. Working end around the anchor point, back up through the loop. 4. Haul — the loop acts as a pulley. 5. Lock off with two half hitches around both strands.
**Mind:** the mid-line loop takes wear; in rope you care about, use the figure-eight version.
**Note:** the graduation knot — loop, turn and half hitches working together.

## The drill

1. **Eyes open:** one knot per evening, tied ten times from memory, checked against this page.
2. **Eyes shut:** once reliable, tie watching the telly, not the cord. Knots are needed in the dark.
3. **Cold test:** week's end, all current knots once each, no peeking. Failures return to stage one.
